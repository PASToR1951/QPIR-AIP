# Getting Started with AIP-PIR

Welcome to the **AIP-PIR Portal** - the Schools Division of Guihulngan City system for preparing Annual Implementation Plans (AIP), filing quarterly Program Implementation Reviews (PIR), reviewing submissions, and producing division reports.

The current portal uses a streamlined, cluster-less review workflow. Clusters still group schools for records, logos, and reports, but the retired Cluster Coordinator step is no longer part of routing.

---

## 1. Sign In and Finish Setup

1. Open the official portal URL in a modern browser.
2. Sign in with your DepEd Google account when enabled, or use the email/password or magic link provided by an administrator.
3. If the portal asks you to change a temporary password, complete that step before continuing.
4. If you are redirected to **Complete Your Profile**, use a desktop or laptop to finish the onboarding wizard.

Pending users can complete onboarding by choosing **School-Based Personnel** or **Division-Based Personnel**, confirming their name and position, and selecting the correct school or division programs. Administrators can still correct roles, account status, assignments, and profile details from **Admin > Users**.

> If sign-in says your account is inactive, pending approval, outside the allowed DepEd domain, or blocked by cookies, contact your Division Admin or SDO IT support.

---

## 2. Know Your Role

Your role controls which workspace, records, and actions you can access.

| Role | Main workspace | What you do |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **School** | Dashboard, AIP, PIR | Prepare school AIPs, file quarterly PIRs after AIP approval, monitor returned items, and download documents. |
| **Division Personnel / Focal Person** | Dashboard, AIP, PIR, Focal Queue | Prepare division-level AIPs/PIRs and recommend or return assigned school submissions as a focal person. |
| **CES Reviewer** | CES Queue, AIP, PIR | Review submissions for the assigned functional division distinction: CES-SGOD, CES-ASDS, or CES-CID. |
| **Superintendent** | Admin and review views | Review CES-owned submissions routed for Superintendent action and monitor division activity. |
| **Admin** | Admin Portal | Manage users, schools, clusters, programs, deadlines, submissions, reports, backups, settings, announcements, FAQs, email templates, and audit logs. |
| **Observer** | Admin Portal, read-only views | Monitor overview data, inspect submissions, view consolidation information, and add allowed management responses without changing workflow records. |
| **Pending** | Onboarding | Complete profile setup or wait for admin action before role-specific tools open. |

## 3. Use the Dashboard First

After sign-in, start from your dashboard.

- Use the global **Reporting Period** picker in the header to choose the reporting year and quarter you want to view.
- Check the cards for your current AIP, PIR, and submission history.
- Review status badges before opening a document.
- Use notifications to jump directly to returned, recommended, approved, session, or announcement-linked records.
- Watch for announcement banners from the admin team.
- On desktop layouts, use the guided onboarding checklist and Help button to tour your role-specific workspace.

---

## 4. Create or Update an AIP

AIPs define the planned activities, targets, implementation months, responsible persons, outputs, and budget details for a program.

1. Open **AIP**.
2. Select the program and reporting year.
3. Complete the profile and strategic alignment details.
4. Enter goals, KPIs, baseline, and quarterly target values.
5. Complete the action plan, activity phases, implementation months, outputs, budget amounts, budget sources, and signature sections.
6. Use autosave while drafting, then review the final step before submitting.
7. Submit the AIP when the document is ready for review.

Only one active AIP is allowed for the same owner, program, and reporting year. Approved AIPs become read-only; if an approved AIP needs correction, use the available edit request flow or contact your administrator.

---

## 5. File a PIR for the Current Quarter

PIRs report accomplishments against activities from an AIP. The portal uses quarterly PIR periods with admin-managed submission windows and configurable month coverage.

1. Open **PIR**.
2. Select the program connected to your AIP.
3. Confirm the reporting year and quarter shown by the form.
4. Review the AIP-derived activities included in that period.
5. Enter indicator quarterly targets, physical accomplishments, financial accomplishments, gaps, actions to address gaps, facilitating factors, hindering factors, and ways forward.
6. Review the final document and submit.

For school users, PIR filing is available only after the related AIP is **Approved**. The system prevents duplicate PIRs for the same AIP and quarter. If the submission window is closed, ask an administrator to review the deadline settings.

## 6. Understand the Review Chain

School-owned AIPs and PIRs move through this current path:

```text
School submits -> Focal recommendation -> CES review -> Approved or Returned
```

Division Personnel assigned as focal persons can recommend a school submission to CES or return it with remarks. CES is one reviewer role in the workflow; the SGOD, OSDS, and CID labels are functional routing distinctions:

| Program division | CES reviewer |
| --- | --- |
| SGOD | CES-SGOD |
| OSDS | CES-ASDS |
| CID | CES-CID |
| Unassigned | CES-CID |

CES-owned AIPs and PIRs route to **For Superintendent Review** so a reviewer does not approve their own submission. Returned documents go back to the author for correction. Open the same AIP or PIR, read the remarks, revise the record, and resubmit it to the appropriate queue.

## 7. Admin Setup Checklist

Admins keep the workflow moving by maintaining the records and settings that users depend on.

- **Users:** create or import accounts, reset passwords, assign roles, review onboarding progress, manage sessions, and update active or pending status.
- **Schools and clusters:** maintain school records, cluster grouping, and logos used in reports and document headers.
- **Programs:** assign functional divisions, focal persons, and program templates so school submissions route correctly.
- **Deadlines:** set quarterly PIR coverage months, opening dates, due dates, and grace periods.
- **Submissions and PIR Review:** monitor workflow status, inspect records, resolve exceptions, and support Superintendent review where applicable.
- **Reports and Consolidation:** generate compliance, workload, budget, accomplishment, factor, AIP funnel, cluster summary, quarterly, and consolidation outputs.
- **Settings:** manage branding, signatories, announcements, email configuration, email templates, and FAQs.
- **Backups and Logs:** review backup tools, audit activity, and log exports for accountability.

## 9. Common Issues

**I cannot find my school.**  
Ask an administrator to confirm that your account is assigned to the correct school and that the school exists in the Admin Schools page.

**My program is missing.**  
Ask an administrator to confirm the program exists, has the correct school-level requirement, is not restricted away from your school, and is assigned to you if your role requires program assignment.

**My dashboard or queue shows the wrong period.**  
Check the global Reporting Period picker in the header. The selected year and quarter filter dashboards, queues, PIR forms, reports, and some admin views.

**My AIP will not submit.**  
Check required fields, signatures, budget entries, KPI/baseline/quarterly target fields, and whether the selected program has focal persons assigned when school routing needs recommendation.

**My PIR says no approved AIP was found.**  
For school submissions, open or submit the approved AIP for the same program and reporting year before filing a PIR.

**My PIR submission window is closed.**  
Ask an administrator to check the quarter's open date, due date, grace period, and month coverage in Deadlines.

**I received a returned document.**  
Open the same AIP or PIR, read the reviewer remarks, make the correction, and resubmit. The portal sends it back to the correct review queue.

**The onboarding wizard says desktop required.**  
Complete profile setup on a desktop or laptop. The wizard uses detailed forms that are intentionally blocked on small screens.

## 10. Getting More Help

Start with the in-app FAQ and the Help button on supported desktop pages. When reporting an issue, include your account email, role, school or division assignment, program name, reporting year and quarter, page URL or screen name, and the approximate time the problem happened.

For account access, role assignment, school records, deadlines, or routing problems, contact your **Division Admin** or SDO IT support.
